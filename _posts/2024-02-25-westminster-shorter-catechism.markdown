<!-- <span style="font-size:2.1em">The Westminster Shorter Catechism</span><br> -->

The original text of 1647, with the Assembly’s proof texts.

<span style="font-weight:bold;font-size:1.2em;">Q1. What is the chief end of man?</span><br>
A1. Man’s chief end is to glorify God,<sup style="font-weight:bold;">1</sup> and to enjoy him for ever.<sup style="font-weight:bold;">2</sup>

1. I Cor. 10:31; Rom. 11:36.
2. Ps. 73:25-28.

<span style="font-weight:bold;font-size:1.2em;">Q2. What rule hath God given to direct us how we may glorify and enjoy him?</span><br>
A2. The word of God, which is contained in the scriptures of the Old and New Testaments,<sup style="font-weight:bold;">1</sup> is the only rule to direct us how we may glorify and enjoy him.<sup style="font-weight:bold;">2</sup>

1. II Tim. 3:16; Eph. 2:20.
2. I John 1:3-4.

<span style="font-weight:bold;font-size:1.2em;">Q3. What do the scriptures principally teach?</span><br>
A3. The scriptures principally teach what man is to believe concerning God, and what duty God requires of man.<sup style="font-weight:bold;">1</sup>

1. II Tim. 1:13; 3:16.

<span style="font-weight:bold;font-size:1.2em;">Q4. What is God?</span><br>
A4. God is a Spirit,<sup style="font-weight:bold;">1</sup> infinite,<sup style="font-weight:bold;">2</sup> eternal,<sup style="font-weight:bold;">3</sup> and unchangeable,<sup style="font-weight:bold;">4</sup> in his being,<sup style="font-weight:bold;">5</sup> wisdom,<sup style="font-weight:bold;">6</sup> power,<sup style="font-weight:bold;">7</sup> holiness,<sup style="font-weight:bold;">8</sup> justice, goodness, and truth.<sup style="font-weight:bold;">9</sup>

1. John 4:24.
2. Job 11:7-9.
3. Ps. 90:2.
4. James 1:17.
5. Exod. 3:14.
6. Ps. 147:5.
7. Rev. 4:8.
8. Rev. 15:4.
9. Exod. 34:6-7.

<span style="font-weight:bold;font-size:1.2em;">Q5. Are there more Gods than one?</span><br>
A5. There is but One only, the living and true God.<sup style="font-weight:bold;">1</sup>

1. Deut. 6:4; Jer. 10:10.

<span style="font-weight:bold;font-size:1.2em;">Q6. How many persons are there in the Godhead?</span><br>
A6. There are three persons in the Godhead; the Father, the Son, and the Holy Ghost; and these three are one God the same in substance, equal in power and glory.<sup style="font-weight:bold;">1</sup>

1. I John 5:7; Matt. 28:19.

<span style="font-weight:bold;font-size:1.2em;">Q7. What are the decrees of God?</span><br>
A7. The decrees of God are, his eternal purpose, according to the counsel of his will, whereby, for his own glory, he hath foreordained whatsoever comes to pass.<sup style="font-weight:bold;">1</sup>

1. Eph. 1:4, 11; Rom. 9:22-23.

<span style="font-weight:bold;font-size:1.2em;">Q8. How doth God execute his decrees?</span><br>
A8. God executeth his decrees in the works of creation and providence.

<span style="font-weight:bold;font-size:1.2em;">Q9. What is the work of creation?</span><br>
A9. The work of creation is, God’s making all things of nothing, by the word of his power, in the space of six days, and all very good.<sup style="font-weight:bold;">1</sup>

1. Gen. 1; Heb. 11:3.

<span style="font-weight:bold;font-size:1.2em;">Q10. How did God create man?</span><br>
A10. God created man male and female, after his own image, in knowledge, righteousness, and holiness, with dominion over the creatures.<sup style="font-weight:bold;">1</sup>

1. Gen. 1:26-28; Col. 3:10; Eph. 4:24.

<span style="font-weight:bold;font-size:1.2em;">Q11. What are God’s works of providence?</span><br>
A11. God’s works of providence are, his most holy,<sup style="font-weight:bold;">1</sup> wise,<sup style="font-weight:bold;">2</sup> and powerful preserving<sup style="font-weight:bold;">3</sup> and governing all his creatures, and all their actions.<sup style="font-weight:bold;">4</sup>

1. Ps. 145:17.
2. Ps. 104:24; Isa. 28:29.
3. Heb. 1:3.
4. Ps. 103:19; Matt. 10:29-31.

<span style="font-weight:bold;font-size:1.2em;">Q12. What special act of providence did God exercise towards man in the estate wherein he was created?</span><br>
A12. When God had created man, he entered into a covenant of life with him, upon condition of perfect obedience; forbidding him to eat of the tree of the knowledge of good and evil, upon the pain of death.<sup style="font-weight:bold;">1</sup>

1. Gal. 3:12; Gen. 2:17.

<span style="font-weight:bold;font-size:1.2em;">Q13. Did our first parents continue in the estate wherein they were created?</span><br>
A13. Our first parents, being left to the freedom of their own will, fell from the estate wherein they were created, by sinning against God.<sup style="font-weight:bold;">1</sup>

1. Gen. 3:6-8, 13; Eccles. 7:29.

<span style="font-weight:bold;font-size:1.2em;">Q14. What is sin?</span><br>
A14. Sin is any want of conformity unto, or transgression of, the law of God.<sup style="font-weight:bold;">1</sup>

1. I John 3:4.

<span style="font-weight:bold;font-size:1.2em;">Q15. What was the sin whereby our first parents fell from the estate wherein they were created?</span><br>
A15. The sin whereby our first parents fell from the estate wherein they were created, was their eating the forbidden fruit.<sup style="font-weight:bold;">1</sup>

1. Gen. 3:6, 12.

<span style="font-weight:bold;font-size:1.2em;">Q16. Did all mankind fall in Adam’s first transgression?</span><br>
A16. The covenant being made with Adam, not only for himself, but for his posterity; all mankind, descending from him by ordinary generation, sinned in him, and fell with him, in his first transgression.<sup style="font-weight:bold;">1</sup>

1. Gen. 2:16-17; Rom. 5:12; I Cor. 15:21-22.

<span style="font-weight:bold;font-size:1.2em;">Q17. Into what estate did the fall bring mankind?</span><br>
A17. The fall brought mankind into an estate of sin and misery.<sup style="font-weight:bold;">1</sup>

1. Rom. 5:12.

<span style="font-weight:bold;font-size:1.2em;">Q18. Wherein consists the sinfulness of that estate whereinto man fell?</span><br>
A18. The sinfulness of that estate whereinto man fell, consists in the guilt of Adam’s first sin, the want of original righteousness, and the corruption of his whole nature, which is commonly called Original Sin; together with all actual transgressions which proceed from it.<sup style="font-weight:bold;">1</sup>

1. Rom. 5:12, 19; 5:10-20; Eph. 2:1-3; James 1:14-15; Matt. 15:19.

<span style="font-weight:bold;font-size:1.2em;">Q19. What is the misery of that estate whereinto man fell?</span><br>
A19. All mankind by their fall lost communion with God,<sup style="font-weight:bold;">1</sup> are under his wrath and curse,<sup style="font-weight:bold;">2</sup> and so made liable to all miseries in this life, to death itself, and to the pains of hell for ever.<sup style="font-weight:bold;">3</sup>

1. Gen. 3:8, 10, 24.
2. Eph. 2:2-3, Gal. 3:10.
3. Lam. 3:39; Rom. 6:23, Matt. 25:41, 46.

<span style="font-weight:bold;font-size:1.2em;">Q20. Did God leave all mankind to perish in the estate of sin and misery?</span><br>
A20. God having, out of his mere good pleasure, from all eternity, elected some to everlasting life,<sup style="font-weight:bold;">1</sup> did enter into a covenant of grace, to deliver them out of the estate of sin and misery, and to bring them into an estate of salvation by a Redeemer.<sup style="font-weight:bold;">2</sup>

1. Eph. 1:4.
2. Rom. 3:20-22; Gal. 3:21-22.

<span style="font-weight:bold;font-size:1.2em;">Q21. Who is the Redeemer of God’s elect?</span><br>
A21. The only Redeemer of God’s elect is the Lord Jesus Christ,<sup style="font-weight:bold;">1</sup> who, being the eternal Son of God, became man,<sup style="font-weight:bold;">2</sup> and so was, and continueth to be, God and man in two distinct natures, and one person, for ever.<sup style="font-weight:bold;">3</sup>

1. I Tim. 2:5-6.
2. John 1:14; Gal. 4:4.
3. Rom. 9:5; Luke 1:35; Col. 2:9; Heb. 7:24-25.

<span style="font-weight:bold;font-size:1.2em;">Q22. How did Christ, being the Son of God, become man?</span><br>
A22. Christ, the Son of God, became man, by taking to himself a true body,<sup style="font-weight:bold;">1</sup> and a reasonable soul,<sup style="font-weight:bold;">2</sup> being conceived by the power of the Holy Ghost, in the womb of the Virgin Mary, and born of her,<sup style="font-weight:bold;">3</sup> yet without sin.<sup style="font-weight:bold;">4</sup>

1. Heb. 2:14, 16; 10:5.
2. Matt. 26:38.
3. Luke 1:27, 31, 35, 42; Gal. 4:4.
4. Heb. 4:15; 7:26.

<span style="font-weight:bold;font-size:1.2em;">Q23. What offices doth Christ execute as our Redeemer?</span><br>
A23. Christ, as our Redeemer, executeth the offices of a prophet, of a priest, and of a king, both in his estate of humiliation and exaltation.<sup style="font-weight:bold;">1</sup>

1. Acts 3:21-22; Heb. 12:25. Cf. II Cor. 13:3; Heb. 5:5-7; 7:25; Ps. 2:6; Isa. 9:6-7; Matt. 21:5; Ps. 2:8-11.

<span style="font-weight:bold;font-size:1.2em;">Q24. How doth Christ execute the office of a prophet?</span><br>
A24. Christ executeth the office of a prophet, in revealing to us, by his word and Spirit, the will of God for our salvation.<sup style="font-weight:bold;">1</sup>

1. John 1:18; I Peter 1:10-12; John 15:15; 20:31.

<span style="font-weight:bold;font-size:1.2em;">Q25. How doth Christ execute the office of a priest?</span><br>
A25. Christ executeth the office of a priest, in his once offering up of himself a sacrifice to satisfy divine justice,<sup style="font-weight:bold;">1</sup> and reconcile us to God,<sup style="font-weight:bold;">2</sup> and in making continual intercession for us.<sup style="font-weight:bold;">3</sup>

1. Heb. 9:14, 28.
2. Heb. 2:17.
3. Heb. 7:24-25.

<span style="font-weight:bold;font-size:1.2em;">Q26. How doth Christ execute the office of a king?</span><br>
A26. Christ executeth the office of a king, in subduing us to himself,<sup style="font-weight:bold;">1</sup> in ruling<sup style="font-weight:bold;">2</sup> and defending us,<sup style="font-weight:bold;">3</sup> and in restraining and conquering all his and our enemies.<sup style="font-weight:bold;">4</sup>

1. Acts 15:14-16.
2. Isa. 33:22.
3. Isa. 32:1-2.
4. I Cor. 15:25, Ps. 110.

<span style="font-weight:bold;font-size:1.2em;">Q27. Wherein did Christ’s humiliation consist?</span><br>
A27. Christ’s humiliation consisted in his being born, and that in a low condition,<sup style="font-weight:bold;">1</sup> made under the law,<sup style="font-weight:bold;">2</sup> undergoing the miseries of this life,<sup style="font-weight:bold;">3</sup> the wrath of God,<sup style="font-weight:bold;">4</sup> and the cursed death of the cross;<sup style="font-weight:bold;">5</sup> in being buried,<sup style="font-weight:bold;">6</sup> and continuing under the power of death for a time.<sup style="font-weight:bold;">7</sup>

1. Luke 2:7.
2. Gal. 4:4.
3. Heb. 12:2-3; Isa. 53:2-3.
4. Luke 22:44; Matt. 27:46.
5. Phil. 2:8.
6. I Cor. 15:3-4.
7. Acts 2:24-27, 31.

<span style="font-weight:bold;font-size:1.2em;">Q28. Wherein consisteth Christ’s exaltation?</span><br>
A28. Christ’s exaltation consisteth in his rising again from the dead on the third day,<sup style="font-weight:bold;">1</sup> in ascending up into heaven,<sup style="font-weight:bold;">2</sup> in sitting at the right hand of God the Father,<sup style="font-weight:bold;">3</sup> and in coming to judge the world at the last day.<sup style="font-weight:bold;">4</sup>

1. I Cor. 15:4.
2. Mark 16:19.
3. Eph. 1:20.
4. Acts 1:11; 17:31.

<span style="font-weight:bold;font-size:1.2em;">Q29. How are we made partakers of the redemption purchased by Christ?</span><br>
A29. We are made partakers of the redemption purchased by Christ, by the effectual application of it to us<sup style="font-weight:bold;">1</sup> by his Holy Spirit.<sup style="font-weight:bold;">2</sup>

1. John 1:11-12.
2. Titus 3:5-6.

<span style="font-weight:bold;font-size:1.2em;">Q30. How doth the Spirit apply to us the redemption purchased by Christ?</span><br>
A30. The Spirit applieth to us the redemption purchased by Christ, by working faith in us,<sup style="font-weight:bold;">1</sup> and thereby uniting us to Christ in our effectual calling.<sup style="font-weight:bold;">2</sup>

1. Eph. 1:13-14; John 6:37, 39; Eph. 2:8.
2. Eph. 3:17, I Cor. 1:9.

<span style="font-weight:bold;font-size:1.2em;">Q31. What is effectual calling?</span><br>
A31. Effectual calling is the work of God’s Spirit,<sup style="font-weight:bold;">1</sup> whereby, convincing us of our sin and misery,<sup style="font-weight:bold;">2</sup> enlightening our minds in the knowledge of Christ,<sup style="font-weight:bold;">3</sup> and renewing our wills,<sup style="font-weight:bold;">4</sup> he doth persuade and enable us to embrace Jesus Christ, freely offered to us in the gospel.<sup style="font-weight:bold;">5</sup>

1. II Tim. 1:9; II Thess. 2:13-14.
2. Acts 2:37.
3. Acts 26:18.
4. Ezek. 36:26-27.
5. John 6:44-45; Phil. 2:13.

<span style="font-weight:bold;font-size:1.2em;">Q32. What benefits do they that are effectually called partake of in this life?</span><br>
A32. They that are effectually called do in this life partake of justification,<sup style="font-weight:bold;">1</sup> adoption,<sup style="font-weight:bold;">2</sup> and sanctification, and the several benefits which in this life do either accompany or flow from them.<sup style="font-weight:bold;">3</sup>

1. Rom. 8:30.
2. Eph. 1:5.
3. I Cor. 1:26, 30.

<span style="font-weight:bold;font-size:1.2em;">Q33. What is justification?</span><br>
A33. Justification is an act of God’s free grace, wherein he pardoneth all our sins,<sup style="font-weight:bold;">1</sup> and accepteth us as righteous in his sight,<sup style="font-weight:bold;">2</sup> only for the righteousness of Christ imputed to us,<sup style="font-weight:bold;">3</sup> and received by faith alone.<sup style="font-weight:bold;">4</sup>

1. Rom. 3:24-25; 4:6-8.
2. II Cor. 5:19, 21.
3. Rom. 5:17-19.
4. Gal. 2:16; Phil. 3:9.

<span style="font-weight:bold;font-size:1.2em;">Q34. What is adoption?</span><br>
A34. Adoption is an act of God’s free grace,<sup style="font-weight:bold;">1</sup> whereby we are received into the number, and have a right to all the privileges of the Sons of God.<sup style="font-weight:bold;">2</sup>

1. I John 3:1.
2. John 1:12; Rom. 8:17.

<span style="font-weight:bold;font-size:1.2em;">Q35. What is sanctification?</span><br>
A35. Sanctification is the work of God’s free grace,<sup style="font-weight:bold;">1</sup> whereby we are renewed in the whole man after the image of God,<sup style="font-weight:bold;">2</sup> and are enabled more and more to die unto sin, and live unto righteousness.<sup style="font-weight:bold;">3</sup>

1. II Thess. 2:13.
2. Eph. 4:23-24.
3. Rom. 6:4, 6.

<span style="font-weight:bold;font-size:1.2em;">Q36. What are the benefits which in this life do accompany or flow from justification, adoption, and sanctification?</span><br>
A36. The benefits which in this life do accompany or flow from justification, adoption, and sanctification, are, assurance of God’s love, peace of conscience,<sup style="font-weight:bold;">1</sup> joy in the Holy Ghost,<sup style="font-weight:bold;">2</sup> increase of grace,<sup style="font-weight:bold;">3</sup> and perseverance therein to the end.<sup style="font-weight:bold;">4</sup>

1. Rom. 5:1-2, 5.
2. Rom. 14:17.
3. Prov. 4:18.
4. I John 5:13; I Pet. 1:5.

<span style="font-weight:bold;font-size:1.2em;">Q37. What benefits do believers receive from Christ at death?</span><br>
A37. The souls of believers are at their death made perfect in holiness,<sup style="font-weight:bold;">1</sup> and do immediately pass into glory;<sup style="font-weight:bold;">2</sup> and their bodies, being still united to Christ,<sup style="font-weight:bold;">3</sup> do rest in their graves<sup style="font-weight:bold;">4</sup> till the resurrection.<sup style="font-weight:bold;">5</sup>

1. Heb. 12:23.
2. II Cor. 5:1, 6, 8; Phil. 1:23; Luke 23:43.
3. I Thess. 4:14.
4. Isa. 57:2.
5. Job 19:26-27.

<span style="font-weight:bold;font-size:1.2em;">Q38. What benefits do believers receive from Christ at the resurrection?</span><br>
A38. At the resurrection, believers being raised up in glory,<sup style="font-weight:bold;">1</sup> shall be openly acknowledged and acquitted in the day of judgment,<sup style="font-weight:bold;">2</sup> and made perfectly blessed in the full enjoying of God<sup style="font-weight:bold;">3</sup> to all eternity.<sup style="font-weight:bold;">4</sup>

1. I Cor. 15:43.
2. Matt. 25:23; 10:32.
3. I John 3:2; I Cor. 13:12.
4. I Thess. 4:17-18.

<span style="font-weight:bold;font-size:1.2em;">Q39. What is the duty which God requireth of man?</span><br>
A39. The duty which God requireth of man, is obedience to his revealed will.<sup style="font-weight:bold;">1</sup>

1. Micah 6:8; I Sam. 15:22.

<span style="font-weight:bold;font-size:1.2em;">Q40. What did God at first reveal to man for the rule of his obedience?</span><br>
A40. The rule which God at first revealed to man for his obedience, was the moral law.<sup style="font-weight:bold;">1</sup>

1. Rom. 2:14-15; 10:5.

<span style="font-weight:bold;font-size:1.2em;">Q41. Where is the moral law summarily comprehended?</span><br>
A41. The moral law is summarily comprehended in the ten commandments.<sup style="font-weight:bold;">1</sup>

1. Deut. 10:4.

<span style="font-weight:bold;font-size:1.2em;">Q42. What is the sum of the ten commandments?</span><br>
A42. The sum of the ten commandments is, To love the Lord our God with all our heart, with all our soul, with all our strength, and with all our mind; and our neighbor as ourselves.<sup style="font-weight:bold;">1</sup>

1. Matt. 22:37-40.

<span style="font-weight:bold;font-size:1.2em;">Q43. What is the preface to the ten commandments?</span><br>
A43. The preface to the ten commandments is in these words, I am the Lord thy God, which have brought thee out of the land of Egypt, out of the house of bondage.<sup style="font-weight:bold;">1</sup>

1. Exod. 20:2.

<span style="font-weight:bold;font-size:1.2em;">Q44. What doth the preface to the ten commandments teach us?</span><br>
A44. The preface to the ten commandments teacheth us, That because God is the Lord, and our God, and Redeemer, therefore we are bound to keep all his commandments.<sup style="font-weight:bold;">1</sup>

1. Luke 1:74-75; I Pet. 1:15-19.

<span style="font-weight:bold;font-size:1.2em;">Q45. Which is the first commandment?</span><br>
A45. The first commandment is, Thou shalt have no other gods before me.<sup style="font-weight:bold;">1</sup>

1. Exod. 20:3.

<span style="font-weight:bold;font-size:1.2em;">Q46. What is required in the first commandment?</span><br>
A46. The first commandment requireth us to know and acknowledge God to be the only true God, and our God;<sup style="font-weight:bold;">1</sup> and to worship and glorify him accordingly.<sup style="font-weight:bold;">2</sup>

1. I Chron. 28:9; Deut. 26:17.
2. Matt. 4:10; Ps. 29:2.

<span style="font-weight:bold;font-size:1.2em;">Q47. What is forbidden in the first commandment?</span><br>
A47. The first commandment forbiddeth the denying,<sup style="font-weight:bold;">1</sup> or not worshipping and glorifying the true God as God,<sup style="font-weight:bold;">2</sup> and our God;<sup style="font-weight:bold;">3</sup> and the giving of that worship and glory to any other, which is due to him alone.<sup style="font-weight:bold;">4</sup>

1. Ps. 14:1.
2. Rom. 1:21.
3. Ps. 81:10-11.
4. Rom. 1:25-26.

<span style="font-weight:bold;font-size:1.2em;">Q48. What are we specially taught by these words, before me in the first commandment?</span><br>
A48. These words before me in the first commandment teach us, That God, who seeth all things, taketh notice of, and is much displeased with, the sin of having any other God.<sup style="font-weight:bold;">1</sup>

1. Ezek. 8:5-18.

<span style="font-weight:bold;font-size:1.2em;">Q49. Which is the second commandment?</span><br>
A49. The second commandment is, Thou shalt not make unto thee any graven image, or any likeness of any thing that is in heaven above, or that is in the earth beneath, or that is in the water under the earth: Thou shalt not bow down thyself to them, nor serve them: for I the Lord thy God am a jealous God, visiting the iniquity of the fathers upon the children unto the third and fourth generation of them that hate me; and showing mercy unto thousands of them that love me, and keep my commandments.<sup style="font-weight:bold;">1</sup>

1. Exod. 20:4-6.

<span style="font-weight:bold;font-size:1.2em;">Q50. What is required in the second commandment?</span><br>
A50. The second commandment requireth the receiving, observing, and keeping pure and entire, all such religious worship and ordinances as God hath appointed in his word.<sup style="font-weight:bold;">1</sup>

1. Deut. 32:46; Matt. 28:20; Acts. 2:42.

<span style="font-weight:bold;font-size:1.2em;">Q51. What is forbidden in the second commandment?</span><br>
A51. The second commandment forbiddeth the worshipping of God by images,<sup style="font-weight:bold;">1</sup> or any other way not appointed in his word.<sup style="font-weight:bold;">2</sup>

1. Deut. 4:15-19; Exod. 32:5, 8.
2. Deut. 12:31-32.

<span style="font-weight:bold;font-size:1.2em;">Q52. What are the reasons annexed to the second commandment?</span><br>
A52. The reasons annexed to the second commandment are, God’s sovereignty over us,<sup style="font-weight:bold;">1</sup> his propriety in us,<sup style="font-weight:bold;">2</sup> and the zeal he hath to his own worship.<sup style="font-weight:bold;">3</sup>

1. Ps. 95:2-3, 6.
2. Ps. 45:11.
3. Exod. 34:13-14.

<span style="font-weight:bold;font-size:1.2em;">Q53. Which is the third commandment?</span><br>
A53. The third commandment is, Thou shalt not take the name of the Lord thy God in vain: for the Lord will not hold him guiltless that taketh his name in vain.<sup style="font-weight:bold;">1</sup>

1. Exod. 20:7.

<span style="font-weight:bold;font-size:1.2em;">Q54. What is required in the third commandment?</span><br>
A54. The third commandment requireth the holy and reverend use of God’s names,<sup style="font-weight:bold;">1</sup> titles,<sup style="font-weight:bold;">2</sup> attributes,<sup style="font-weight:bold;">3</sup> ordinances,<sup style="font-weight:bold;">4</sup> word,<sup style="font-weight:bold;">5</sup> and works.<sup style="font-weight:bold;">6</sup>

1. Matt. 6:9; Deut. 28:58.
2. Ps. 68:4.
3. Rev. 15:3-4.
4. Mal. 1:11, 14.
5. Ps. 138:1-2.
6. Job 36:24.

<span style="font-weight:bold;font-size:1.2em;">Q55. What is forbidden in the third commandment?</span><br>
A55. The third commandment forbiddeth all profaning or abusing anything whereby God maketh himself known.<sup style="font-weight:bold;">1</sup>

1. Mal. 1:6-7, 12; 2:2; 3:14.

<span style="font-weight:bold;font-size:1.2em;">Q56. What is the reason annexed to the third commandment?</span><br>
A56. The reason annexed to the third commandment is, That however the breakers of this commandment may escape punishment from men, yet the Lord our God will not suffer them to escape his righteous judgment.<sup style="font-weight:bold;">1</sup>

1. I Sam. 2:12, 17, 22, 29; 3:13; Deut. 28:58-59.

<span style="font-weight:bold;font-size:1.2em;">Q57. Which is the fourth commandment?</span><br>
A57. The fourth commandment is, Remember the sabbath-day, to keep it holy. Six days shalt thou labour, and do all thy work: but the seventh day is the sabbath of the Lord thy God: in it thou shalt not do any work, thou, nor thy son, nor thy daughter, thy man-servant, nor thy maid-servant, nor thy cattle, nor thy stranger that is within thy gates: For in six days the Lord made heaven and earth, the sea, and all that in them is, and rested the seventh day: wherefore the Lord blessed the sabbath day, and hallowed it.<sup style="font-weight:bold;">1</sup>

1. Exod. 20:8-11.

<span style="font-weight:bold;font-size:1.2em;">Q58. What is required in the fourth commandment?</span><br>
A58. The fourth commandment requireth the keeping holy to God such set times as he hath appointed in his word; expressly one whole day in seven, to be a holy sabbath to himself.<sup style="font-weight:bold;">1</sup>

1. Deut. 5:12-14.

<span style="font-weight:bold;font-size:1.2em;">Q59. Which day of the seven hath God appointed to be the weekly sabbath?</span><br>
A59. From the beginning of the world to the resurrection of Christ, God appointed the seventh day of the week to be the weekly sabbath; and the first day of the week ever since, to continue to the end of the world, which is the Christian sabbath.<sup style="font-weight:bold;">1</sup>

1. Gen. 2:2-3; I Cor. 16:1-2; Acts 20:7.

<span style="font-weight:bold;font-size:1.2em;">Q60. How is the sabbath to be sanctified?</span><br>
A60. The sabbath is to be sanctified by a holy resting all that day,<sup style="font-weight:bold;">1</sup> even from such worldly employments and recreations as are lawful on other days;<sup style="font-weight:bold;">2</sup> and spending the whole time in the publick and private exercises of God’s worship,<sup style="font-weight:bold;">3</sup> except so much as is to be taken up in the works of necessity and mercy.<sup style="font-weight:bold;">4</sup>

1. Exod. 20:8, 10; 16:25-28.
2. Neh. 13:15-19, 21-22.
3. Luke 4:16; Acts 20:7; Ps. 92 title; Isa. 66:23.
4. Matt. 12:1-31.

<span style="font-weight:bold;font-size:1.2em;">Q61. What is forbidden in the fourth commandment?</span><br>
A61. The fourth commandment forbiddeth the omission or careless performance of the duties required,<sup style="font-weight:bold;">1</sup> and the profaning the day by idleness,<sup style="font-weight:bold;">2</sup> or doing that which is in itself sinful,<sup style="font-weight:bold;">3</sup> or by unnecessary thoughts, words, or works, about our worldly employments or recreations.<sup style="font-weight:bold;">4</sup>

1. Ezek. 22:26; Amos 8:5; Mal. 1:13.
2. Acts 20:7, 9.
3. Ezek. 23:38.
4. Jer. 17:24-26; Isa. 58:13.

<span style="font-weight:bold;font-size:1.2em;">Q62. What are the reasons annexed to the fourth commandment?</span><br>
A62. The reasons annexed to the fourth commandment are, God’s allowing us six days of the week for our own employments,<sup style="font-weight:bold;">1</sup> his challenging a special propriety in the seventh, his own example, and his blessing the sabbath-day.<sup style="font-weight:bold;">2</sup>

1. Exod. 20:9.
2. Exod. 20:11.

<span style="font-weight:bold;font-size:1.2em;">Q63. Which is the fifth commandment?</span><br>
A63. The fifth commandment is, Honour thy father and thy mother; that thy days may be long upon the land which the Lord thy God giveth thee.<sup style="font-weight:bold;">1</sup>

1. Exod. 20:12.

<span style="font-weight:bold;font-size:1.2em;">Q64. What is required in the fifth commandment?</span><br>
A64. The fifth commandment requireth the preserving the honour, and performing the duties, belonging to every one in their several places and relations, as superiors,<sup style="font-weight:bold;">1</sup> inferiors,<sup style="font-weight:bold;">2</sup> or equals.<sup style="font-weight:bold;">3</sup>

1. Eph. 5:21.
2. I Pet. 2:17.
3. Rom. 12:10.

<span style="font-weight:bold;font-size:1.2em;">Q65. What is the forbidden in the fifth commandment?</span><br>
A65. The fifth commandment forbiddeth the neglecting of, or doing anything against, the honour and duty which belongeth to every one in their several places and relations.<sup style="font-weight:bold;">1</sup>

1. Matt. 15:4-6; Ezek. 34:2-4; Rom. 13:8.

<span style="font-weight:bold;font-size:1.2em;">Q66. What is the reason annexed to the fifth commandment?</span><br>
A66. The reason annexed to the fifth commandment, is a promise of long life and prosperity (as far as it shall serve for God’s glory and their own good) to all such as keep this commandment.<sup style="font-weight:bold;">1</sup>

1. Deut. 5:16; Eph. 6:2-3.

<span style="font-weight:bold;font-size:1.2em;">Q67. Which is the sixth commandment?</span><br>
A67. The sixth commandment is, Thou shalt not kill.<sup style="font-weight:bold;">1</sup>

1. Exod. 20:13.

<span style="font-weight:bold;font-size:1.2em;">Q68. What is required in the sixth commandment?</span><br>
A68. The sixth commandment requireth all lawful endeavours to preserve our own life,<sup style="font-weight:bold;">1</sup> and the life of others.<sup style="font-weight:bold;">2</sup>

1. Eph. 5:28-29.
2. I Kings 18:4.

<span style="font-weight:bold;font-size:1.2em;">Q69. What is forbidden in the sixth commandment?</span><br>
A69. The sixth commandment forbiddeth the taking away of our own life, or the life of our neighbour unjustly, or whatsoever tendeth thereunto.<sup style="font-weight:bold;">1</sup>

1. Acts 16:28; Gen. 9:6.

<span style="font-weight:bold;font-size:1.2em;">Q70. Which is the seventh commandment?</span><br>
A70. The seventh commandment is, Thou shalt not commit adultery.<sup style="font-weight:bold;">1</sup>

1. Exod. 20:14.

<span style="font-weight:bold;font-size:1.2em;">Q71. What is required in the seventh commandment?</span><br>
A71. The seventh commandment requireth the preservation of our own and our neighbor’s chastity, in heart, speech, and behaviour.<sup style="font-weight:bold;">1</sup>

1. I Cor. 7:2-3, 5, 34, 36; Col. 4:6; I Pet. 3:2.

<span style="font-weight:bold;font-size:1.2em;">Q72. What is forbidden in the seventh commandment?</span><br>
A72. The seventh commandment forbiddeth all unchaste thoughts, words, and actions.<sup style="font-weight:bold;">1</sup>

1. Matt. 15:19; 5:28; Eph. 5:3-4.

<span style="font-weight:bold;font-size:1.2em;">Q73. Which is the eighth commandment?</span><br>
A73. The eighth commandment is, Thou shalt not steal.<sup style="font-weight:bold;">1</sup>

1. Exod. 20:15.

<span style="font-weight:bold;font-size:1.2em;">Q74. What is required in the eighth commandment?</span><br>
A74. The eighth commandment requireth the lawful procuring and furthering the wealth and outward estate of ourselves and others.<sup style="font-weight:bold;">1</sup>

1. Gen. 30:30; I Tim. 5:8; Lev. 25:35; Deut. 22:1-5; Exod. 23:4-5; Gen. 47:14, 20.

<span style="font-weight:bold;font-size:1.2em;">Q75. What is forbidden in the eighth commandment?</span><br>
A75. The eighth commandment forbiddeth whatsoever doth or may unjustly hinder our own or our neighbour’s wealth or outward estate.<sup style="font-weight:bold;">1</sup>

1. Prov. 21:17; 23:20-21; 28:19; Eph. 4:28.

<span style="font-weight:bold;font-size:1.2em;">Q76. What is the ninth commandment?</span><br>
A76. The ninth commandment is, Thou shalt not bear false witness against thy neighbour.<sup style="font-weight:bold;">1</sup>

1. Exod. 20:16.

<span style="font-weight:bold;font-size:1.2em;">Q77. What is required in the ninth commandment?</span><br>
A77. The ninth commandment requireth the maintaining and promoting of truth between man and man,<sup style="font-weight:bold;">1</sup> and of our own and our neighbour’s good name,<sup style="font-weight:bold;">2</sup> especially in witness-bearing.<sup style="font-weight:bold;">3</sup>

1. Zech. 8:16.
2. III John 12.
3. Prov. 14:5, 25.

<span style="font-weight:bold;font-size:1.2em;">Q78. What is forbidden in the ninth commandment?</span><br>
A78. The ninth commandment forbiddeth whatsoever is prejudicial to truth, or injurious to our own or our neighbour’s good name.<sup style="font-weight:bold;">1</sup>

1. I Sam. 17:28; Lev. 19:16; Ps. 15:3.

<span style="font-weight:bold;font-size:1.2em;">Q79. Which is the tenth commandment?</span><br>
A79. The tenth commandment is, Thou shalt not covet thy neighbour’s house, thou shalt not covet thy neighbour’s wife, nor his manservant, nor his maidservant, nor his ox, nor his ass, nor any thing that is thy neighbour’s.<sup style="font-weight:bold;">1</sup>

1. Exod. 20:17.

<span style="font-weight:bold;font-size:1.2em;">Q80. What is required in the tenth commandment?</span><br>
A80. The tenth commandment requireth full contentment with our own condition,<sup style="font-weight:bold;">1</sup> with a right and charitable frame of spirit toward our neighbour, and all this is his.<sup style="font-weight:bold;">2</sup>

1. Heb. 13:5; I Tim. 6:6.
2. Job 31:29; Rom. 12:15; I Tim. 1:5; I Cor. 13:4-7.

<span style="font-weight:bold;font-size:1.2em;">Q81. What is forbidden in the tenth commandment?</span><br>
A81. The tenth commandment forbiddeth all discontentment with our own estate,<sup style="font-weight:bold;">1</sup> envying or grieving at the good of our neighbour,<sup style="font-weight:bold;">2</sup> and all inordinate motions and affections to any thing that is his.<sup style="font-weight:bold;">3</sup>

1. I Kings 21:4; Esther 5:13; I Cor. 10:10.
2. Gal. 5:26; James 3:14, 16.
3. Rom. 7:7-8; 13:9; Deut. 5:21.

<span style="font-weight:bold;font-size:1.2em;">Q82. Is any man able perfectly to keep the commandments of God?</span><br>
A82. No mere man since the fall is able in this life perfectly to keep the commandments of God,<sup style="font-weight:bold;">1</sup> but doth daily break them in thought, word, and deed.<sup style="font-weight:bold;">2</sup>

1. Eccles. 7:20; I John 1:8, 10; Gal. 5:17.
2. Gen. 6:5; 8:21; Rom. 3:9-21; James 3:2-13.

<span style="font-weight:bold;font-size:1.2em;">Q83. Are all transgression of the law equally heinous?</span><br>
A83. Some sins in themselves, and by reason of several aggravations, are more heinous in the sight of God than others.<sup style="font-weight:bold;">1</sup>

1. Ezek. 8:6, 13, 15; I John 5:16; Ps. 78:17, 32, 56.

<span style="font-weight:bold;font-size:1.2em;">Q84. What doth every sin deserve?</span><br>
A84. Every sin deserveth God’s wrath and curse, both in this life, and that which is to come.<sup style="font-weight:bold;">1</sup>

1. Eph. 5:6; Gal. 3:10; Lam. 3:39; Matt. 25:41.

<span style="font-weight:bold;font-size:1.2em;">Q85. What doth God require of us, that we may escape his wrath and curse due to us for sin?</span><br>
A85. To escape the wrath and curse of God due to us for sin, God requireth of us faith in Jesus Christ, repentance unto life,<sup style="font-weight:bold;">1</sup> with the diligent use of all the outward means whereby Christ communicateth to us the benefits of redemption.<sup style="font-weight:bold;">2</sup>

1. Acts. 20:21.
2. Prov. 2:1-5; 8:33-36; Isa. 55:3.

<span style="font-weight:bold;font-size:1.2em;">Q86. What is faith in Jesus Christ?</span><br>
A86. Faith in Jesus Christ is a saving grace,<sup style="font-weight:bold;">1</sup> whereby we receive and rest upon him alone for salvation, as he is offered to us in the gospel.<sup style="font-weight:bold;">2</sup>

1. Heb. 10:39.
2. John 1:12; Isa. 26:3-4; Phil. 3:9; Gal. 2:16.

<span style="font-weight:bold;font-size:1.2em;">Q87. What is repentance unto life?</span><br>
A87. Repentance unto life is a saving grace,<sup style="font-weight:bold;">1</sup> whereby a sinner, out of a true sense of his sin,<sup style="font-weight:bold;">2</sup> and apprehension of the mercy of God in Christ,<sup style="font-weight:bold;">3</sup> doth, with grief and hatred of his sin, turn from it unto God,<sup style="font-weight:bold;">4</sup> with full purpose of, and endeavour after, new obedience.<sup style="font-weight:bold;">5</sup>

1. Acts. 11:18.
2. Acts. 2:37-38.
3. Joel 2:12; Jer. 3:22.
4. Jer. 31:18-19; Ezek. 36:31.
5. II Cor. 7:11; Isa. 1:16-17.

<span style="font-weight:bold;font-size:1.2em;">Q88. What are the outward means whereby Christ communicateth to us the benefits of redemption?</span><br>
A88. The outward and ordinary means whereby Christ communicateth to us the benefits of redemption, are his ordinances, especially the word, sacraments, and prayer; all which are made effectual to the elect for salvation.<sup style="font-weight:bold;">1</sup>

1. Matt. 28:19-20; Acts 2:42, 46-47.

<span style="font-weight:bold;font-size:1.2em;">Q89. How is the word made effectual to salvation?</span><br>
A89. The Spirit of God maketh the reading, but especially the preaching of the word, an effectual means of convincing and converting sinners, and of building them up in holiness and comfort, through faith, unto salvation.<sup style="font-weight:bold;">1</sup>

1. Neh. 8:8; I Cor. 14:24-25; Acts 26:18; Ps. 19:8; Acts 20:32; Rom. 15:4; II Tim. 3:15-17; Rom. 10:13-17; 1:16.

<span style="font-weight:bold;font-size:1.2em;">Q90. How is the word to be read and heard, that it may become effectual to salvation?</span><br>
A90. The word may become effectual to salvation, we must attend thereunto with diligence,<sup style="font-weight:bold;">1</sup> preparation,<sup style="font-weight:bold;">2</sup> and prayer;<sup style="font-weight:bold;">3</sup> receive it with faith and love,<sup style="font-weight:bold;">4</sup> lay it up in our hearts,<sup style="font-weight:bold;">5</sup> and practice it in our lives.<sup style="font-weight:bold;">6</sup>

1. Prov. 8:34.
2. I Pet. 2:1-2.
3. Ps. 119:18.
4. Heb. 4:2; II Thess. 2:10.
5. Ps. 119:11.
6. Luke 8:15; James 1:25.

<span style="font-weight:bold;font-size:1.2em;">Q91. How do the sacraments become effectual means of salvation?</span><br>
A91. The sacraments become effectual means of salvation, not from any virtue in them, or in him that doth administer them; but only by the blessing of Christ,<sup style="font-weight:bold;">1</sup> and the working of his Spirit in them that by faith receive them.<sup style="font-weight:bold;">2</sup>

1. I Pet. 3:21; Matt. 3:11; I Cor. 3:6-7.
2. I Cor. 12:13.

<span style="font-weight:bold;font-size:1.2em;">Q92. What is a sacrament?</span><br>
A92. A sacrament is an holy ordinance instituted by Christ, wherein, by sensible signs, Christ, and the benefits of the new covenant, are represented, sealed, and applied to believers.<sup style="font-weight:bold;">1</sup>

1. Gen. 17:7, 10; Exod. 12; I Cor. 11:23, 26.

<span style="font-weight:bold;font-size:1.2em;">Q93. Which are the sacraments of the New Testament?</span><br>
A93. The sacraments of the New Testament are, Baptism,<sup style="font-weight:bold;">2</sup> and the Lord’s supper.<sup style="font-weight:bold;">2</sup>

1. Matt. 28:19.
2. Matt. 26:26-28.

<span style="font-weight:bold;font-size:1.2em;">Q94. What is baptism?</span><br>
A94. Baptism is a sacrament, wherein the washing with water in the name of the Father, and of the Son, and of the Holy Ghost,<sup style="font-weight:bold;">1</sup> doth signify and seal our ingrafting into Christ, and partaking of the benefits of the covenant of grace, and our engagement to be the Lord’s.<sup style="font-weight:bold;">2</sup>

1. Matt. 28:19.
2. Rom. 6:4; Gal. 3:27.

<span style="font-weight:bold;font-size:1.2em;">Q95. To whom is baptism to be administered?</span><br>
A95. Baptism is not to be administered to any that are out of the visible church, till they profess their faith in Christ, and obedience to him;<sup style="font-weight:bold;">1</sup> but the infants of such as are members of the visible church are to be baptized.<sup style="font-weight:bold;">2</sup>

1. Acts 8:36-37; 2:38.
2. Acts 2:38-39; Gen. 17:10. Cf. Col. 2:11-12; I Cor. 7:14.

<span style="font-weight:bold;font-size:1.2em;">Q96. What is the Lord’s supper?</span><br>
A96. The Lord’s Supper is a sacrament, wherein, by giving and receiving bread and wine, according to Christ’s appointment, his death is showed forth; and the worth receivers are, not after a corporal and carnal manner, but by faith, made partakers of his body and blood, with all his benefits, to their spiritual nourishment, and growth in grace.<sup style="font-weight:bold;">1</sup>

1. I Cor. 11:23-26; 10:16.

<span style="font-weight:bold;font-size:1.2em;">Q97. What is required to be the worthy receiving of the Lord’s supper?</span><br>
A97. It is required of them that would worthily partake of the Lord’s supper, that they examine themselves of their knowledge to discern the Lord’s body,<sup style="font-weight:bold;">1</sup> of their faith to feed upon him,<sup style="font-weight:bold;">2</sup> of their repentance,<sup style="font-weight:bold;">3</sup> love,<sup style="font-weight:bold;">4</sup> and new obedience;<sup style="font-weight:bold;">5</sup> lest, coming unworthily, they eat and drink judgment to themselves.<sup style="font-weight:bold;">6</sup>

1. I Cor. 11:28-29.
2. II Cor. 13:5.
3. I Cor. 11:31.
4. I Cor. 10:16-17.
5. I Cor. 5:7-8.
6. I Cor. 11:28-29.

<span style="font-weight:bold;font-size:1.2em;">Q98. What is prayer?</span><br>
A98. Prayer is an offering up of our desires unto God,<sup style="font-weight:bold;">1</sup> for things agreeable to his will,<sup style="font-weight:bold;">2</sup> in the name of Christ,<sup style="font-weight:bold;">3</sup> with confession of our sins,<sup style="font-weight:bold;">4</sup> and thankful acknowledgment of his mercies.<sup style="font-weight:bold;">5</sup>

1. Ps. 62:8.
2. I John 5:14.
3. John 16:23.
4. Ps. 32:5-6; Dan. 9:4.
5. Phil. 4:6.

<span style="font-weight:bold;font-size:1.2em;">Q99. What rule hath God given for our direction in prayer?</span><br>
A99. The whole word of God is of use to direct us in prayer;<sup style="font-weight:bold;">1</sup> but the special rule of direction is that form of prayer which Christ taught his disciples, commonly called The Lord’s prayer.<sup style="font-weight:bold;">2</sup>

1. I John 5:14.
2. Matt. 6:9-13. Cf. Luke 11:2-4.

<span style="font-weight:bold;font-size:1.2em;">Q100. What doth the preface of the Lord’s prayer teach us?</span><br>
A100. The preface of the Lord’s prayer (which is, Our Father which art in heaven)<sup style="font-weight:bold;">1</sup> teacheth us to draw near to God with all holy reverence and confidence, as children to a father, able and ready to help us;<sup style="font-weight:bold;">2</sup> and that we should pray with and for others.<sup style="font-weight:bold;">3</sup>

1. Matt. 6:9.
2. Rom. 8:15; Luke 11:13.
3. Acts. 12:5; I Tim. 2:1-2.

<span style="font-weight:bold;font-size:1.2em;">Q101. What do we pray for in the first petition?</span><br>
A101. In the first petition (which is, Hallowed be thy name)<sup style="font-weight:bold;">1</sup> we pray, That God would enable us and others to glorify him in all that whereby he maketh himself known;<sup style="font-weight:bold;">2</sup> and that he would dispose all things to his own glory.<sup style="font-weight:bold;">3</sup>

1. Matt. 6:9.
2. Ps. 67:2-3.
3. Ps. 83.

<span style="font-weight:bold;font-size:1.2em;">Q102. What do we pray for in the second petition?</span><br>
A102. In the second petition (which is, Thy kingdom come)<sup style="font-weight:bold;">1</sup> we pray, That Satan’s kingdom may be destroyed;<sup style="font-weight:bold;">2</sup> and that the kingdom of grace may be advanced,<sup style="font-weight:bold;">3</sup> ourselves and others brought into it, and kept in it;<sup style="font-weight:bold;">4</sup> and that the kingdom of glory may be hastened.<sup style="font-weight:bold;">5</sup>

1. Matt. 6:10.
2. Ps. 68:1, 18.
3. Rev. 12:10-11.
4. II Thess. 3:1; Rom. 10:1; John 17:9, 20.
5. Rev. 22:20.

<span style="font-weight:bold;font-size:1.2em;">Q103. What do we pray for in the third petition?</span><br>
A103. In the third petition (which is, Thy will be done in earth, as it is in heaven)<sup style="font-weight:bold;">1</sup> we pray, That God, by his grace, would make us able and willing to know, obey, and submit to his will in all things,<sup style="font-weight:bold;">2</sup> as the angels do in heaven.<sup style="font-weight:bold;">3</sup>

1. Matt. 6:10.
2. Ps. 67; 119:36; Matt. 26:39; II Sam. 15:25; Job 1:21.
3. Ps. 103:20-21.

<span style="font-weight:bold;font-size:1.2em;">Q104. What do we pray for in the fourth petition?</span><br>
A104. In the fourth petition (which is, Give us this day our daily bread)<sup style="font-weight:bold;">1</sup>, we pray, That of God’s free gift we may receive a competent portion of the good things of this life, and enjoy his blessing with them.<sup style="font-weight:bold;">2</sup>

1. Matt. 6:11.
2. Prov. 30:8-9; Gen. 28:20; I Tim. 4:4-5.

<span style="font-weight:bold;font-size:1.2em;">Q105. What do we pray for in the fifth petition?</span><br>
A105. In the fifth petition (which is, And forgive us our debts, as we forgive our debtors)<sup style="font-weight:bold;">1</sup> we pray, That God, for Christ’s sake, would freely pardon all our sins;<sup style="font-weight:bold;">2</sup> which we are the rather encouraged to ask, because by his grace we are enabled from the heart to forgive others.<sup style="font-weight:bold;">3</sup>

1. Matt. 6:12.
2. Ps. 51:1-2, 7, 9; Dan. 9:17-19.
3. Luke 11:4, Matt. 18:35.

<span style="font-weight:bold;font-size:1.2em;">Q106. What do we pray for in the sixth petition?</span><br>
A106. In the sixth petition (which is, And lead us not into temptation, but deliver us from evil)<sup style="font-weight:bold;">1</sup> we pray, that God would either keep us from being tempted to sin,<sup style="font-weight:bold;">2</sup> or support and deliver us when we are tempted.<sup style="font-weight:bold;">3</sup>

1. Matt. 6:13.
2. Matt. 26:41.
3. II Cor. 12:7-8.

<span style="font-weight:bold;font-size:1.2em;">Q107. What doth the conclusion the Lord’s prayer teach us?</span><br>
A107. The conclusion of the Lord’s prayer (which is, For thine is the kingdom, and the power, and the glory, for ever, Amen)<sup style="font-weight:bold;">1</sup> teacheth us to take our encouragement in prayer from God only,<sup style="font-weight:bold;">2</sup> and in our prayers to praise him, ascribing kingdom, power, and glory to him.<sup style="font-weight:bold;">3</sup> And, in testimony of our desire, and assurance to be heard, we say, Amen.<sup style="font-weight:bold;">4</sup>

1. Matt. 6:13.
2. Dan. 9:4, 7-9, 16-19.
3. I Chron. 29:10-13.
4. I Cor. 14:16; Rev. 22:20-21.
